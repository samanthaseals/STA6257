Keep only subjid (their identifier in the study), VisitDate (the date of their clinic visit), age (in years), BPmeds (1=yes, 0=no for hypertension medication), sbp (systolic blood pressure in mmHg), and pa3cat (0=poor health, 1=intermediate health, 2=ideal health, for physical activity as defined by the American Heart Association) in each dataset.

11. (Challenge!) Combine the three graphs into one that stacks the scatterplots. (Hint: look up the ggpubr package.)

12. (Challenge!) Summarize the data – create a Table 1 (see the PDF of the linked paper on Canvas). (Hint: the table1 package may be helpful.) Include average age, average time since V1, average systolic blood pressure, counts and percentages for those that are on hypertension medication, counts and percentages for health as defined by physical activity, and counts and percentages for those starting hypertension medication since the last clinic visit. Split the table by clinic visit and do not include an “overall” column. (Ask yourself why the “overall” column is not appropriate for this dataset.)
